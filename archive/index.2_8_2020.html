    Martin Jinye Zhang

    martinjzhang at gmail.com [Github] [Google Scholar] [Twitter]

    Hello! I am Martin, a postdoc at Harvard School of Public Health working with Prof. Alkes Price on statistical genetics. Currently, I am interested in analyzing the UK biobank whole exome sequencing (WES) data, and potential methods for combining GWAS with single-cell RNA-seq. Before that, I did my PhD at Stanford with Prof. David Tse and Prof. James Zou on statistics, machine learning, and computational biology. Some topics include empirical Bayes, multiple hypothesis testing, multi-armed bandits, single-cell RNA-seq.

    I publish under Martin Jinye Zhang (and its variations). I also go under Jinye Zhang (张金野).

    News:

    • 2019.12 Our paper on identifying aging signatures using the Tabula Muris Senis data is available online.

    • 2019.11 Our paper on experimental design for single-cell RNA-seq was accepted by Nature Communications. I also contributed to the Tabular Muris Senis paper revision.

    Softwares

    • adafdr: covariate-adaptive multiple testing.

    • sceb: sequencing-depth aware estimators for single-cell RNA-seq analysis via empirical Bayes.

    • Meddit: an almost linear algorithm for computing the medoid for a set of n points via adaptive sampling.

    • contrastive: a python library for performing unsupervised machine learning on datasets with learning (e.g. PCA) in contrastive settings, where one is interested in patterns (e.g. clusters or clines) that exist one dataset, but not the other.
